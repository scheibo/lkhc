Report

Another successful Low-Key series is in the bag!

Congratulations to series winners Brian Lucido, Janet Martinez, and Western Wheelers, who win the men's, women's and team standings. But don't forget the other standings. Karis McFarlane won the endurance award by a slim 5 minute margin from Kley Cardona. Brad Ford won the "most improved during series", taking that lead with a strong 115 points in the final climb. Richard Contreras was "most improved since last year" with an impressive 12.9 point improvement, barely beating out Shance Ordell, with Brian Edwards also improving by more than 10 points to finish third. Will Von Kaenel took advantage of volunteer credit to lock down the most consistent rider standings, just edging out Russ McCrary and Eric Chi. And Ireland's Melanie Spath scored the top result of the series with her spectacular record-breaking, Strava-QOM of 1:20 up Mt Hamilton. Andre Swart repeated as top junior, ahead of Skyler "3.0" Colwell.

Thanks to all of our riders, all of our volunteers, all of our coordinators, and all of our supporters out there for helping us complete another fun and exciting series. We hope to see you there for the end-of-season party @ Sunnyale Sports Basement on 17 Dec, then for one of our 2011 fun happenings.
