Scoring Scheme
Points for a stage are a percentage of the fastest rider's speed. Non-participation in a stage counts as zero points.

Net points are the sum of the top n scores for a rider, where n is half the number of stages held to date, rounded down.

The score for a ride coordinator on a stage he leads is calculated as an average of his scores for other stages. If an individual rode in r and coordinated c stages, the number of stages averaged to yield the score for the coordinated stages is n minus the lesser of c and n/2 (rounded down).

If two riders are tied on the sum of their top n stages, the tie is broken by summing their top n+m stages, where m is the smallest postive integer which will break the tie.

Team scores are the sum of the scores of the top two finishers for the team in a given week. The net score for a team is the sum of its scores over all weeks.
