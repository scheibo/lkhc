Report

And so it is done. 2014 was another year of a spectacularly successful Low-Key Hillclimbs, and for that we thank our great coordinators, volunteers, and riders.

Congratulations to our overall category leaders for 2014: McLovely Brown led the women over the Sisters and Misters pair of Amy Cameron and Janet Gardner, both of whom posted truly excellent scores at Mount Hamilton, but McLovely was able to sit on her existing results to hold the overall lead. In the men, Stefano Profumo held off David Collet's dirt-fueled charge at the Marin Headlands, with Stefano's Squadra SF teammate Chris Evans in a strong 3rd place made even stronger by his spectacular win on Mount Hamilton. Bill Bushnell was our sole hybrid-electric participant this year, a surprise given the proliferation of these vehicles. Gary Gellin was our top runner, with Bill Bushnell a remarkably close second for his hike at the Marin Headlands, and Niall Fitzgerald 3rd based on his strong climb of Welch Creek.

In the team competition, it was the amazing Sisters and Misters repeating their victory from last year, albeit with a more comfortable margin, over Squadra SF who overtook The Brown Zone for second place with their top 2 at Hamilton.

In the new "megateam" ranking (every score counts) it was Sisters and Misters finishing with a healthy gap due to their consistent, massive turn-out, especially among the women. Indeed, the Sisters had 4 of the top 8 women in the overall.

Notable this year was two surviving members of the elite 100% club: Rich Hill and Frank Paysen riding or volunteering at every one of the 9 climbs in the series.

Thanks once again to everyone for making this such an enjoyable and successful series!!!
