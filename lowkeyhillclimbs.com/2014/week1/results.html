Report

Week one of the Low-Key Hillclimbs was a red-hot success, with some spectacular performances. Each year it gets harder to break Low-Key records, each year it gets harder to get Strava KOMs, yet today's climb saw both shattered in both the men's and women's divisions, with 2nd-best-ever times in the hybrid electric and tandem divisions.

Stanford School of Medicine's Nick Bax scored a convincing first in the men's overall, finishing 33 seconds ahead of Chris Phipps' time from 2010. In the women, it was Beth Reid (formerly Beth Heiden) who toppled Laura Stern's 19-year-old record from 1995 by a remarkable 1:40. McLovely Brown, meanwhile, broke her existing Strava QOM to put herself at the top of that list. Beth now holds two Low-Key records for Montebello, also as part of the best-ever mixed tandem result with her husband Russell from 2010.

Bill Bushnell, riding for Western Wheelers this year, took the top spot in the Hybrid Electric standings, putting up the 2nd best time on Low-Key record for hybrid-electric, only the 2nd time the 18-minute barrier has been broken. The top tandem team, Emma and Jonathan Dixon, lead a group of 3 mixed tandems to produce the 2nd best of 15 mixed tandem times on record.

A very solid turn-out of 16 women were led by Beth, McLovely, with Helen Casabona in 3rd. On the men's side, Nick was followed by the Squadra SF pair of Stefano Profumo and Chris Evans, both of whom broke 27 minutes. Our tandem podium included the Dixons at the top spot, Rich and Kim Hill next, and Pat Callahan and Ping Yin next.

One feature of this week's climb was the exceptional heat, resulting in a shattering not only of Low-Key and Strava records for climbing times, but also for water consumption at the finish, where we went through more than double that on a typical Low-Key week. We try to provision for rider needs, but we kindly request riders take what they need for immediate use and for the descent to make sure there's still some for the later finishers.

In the team standings, it was Squadra Ovest to take the top spot for thw week, followed by perennial Low-Key powerhouses The Brown Zone and LGBRC. Toward the overall standings volunteer credits will be counted as riders who volunteered this week climb later in the series.

Thanks to James and to the incredible volunteer crew this week for working so hard to help get Low-Key off to such an amazing start!

This week's climb was a return to "Low-Key" by our friend Adam Tow. See his blog post here!
