Report

It was a gorgeous opening day for Low-Key 2013, with warm temperatures for Low-Key season.

While super-junior and 2012 Low-Key overall champion Adrien Costa wasn't able to make the start due to a broken clavical suffered this past week in training, Jason Saltzman of the Team Specialized Racing Juniors kept the junior streak alive by dropping 2013 national master's road champion Chris Phipps with a late attack to take the victory for week 1 among the men, finishing in 26:03. Phipps still holds the Low-Key record with his 25:32 from 2010.

In the women's standings, it was first-time Low-Keyer Jane Despas who finished first. Laura Stern still holds the record with her 31:42 from 1995, while 2012 Montebello winner and second all-time Lisa Penzel had to turn back before the finish due to complications from oral surgery this past week: Lisa proved how tough she is by even starting the climb.

For tandems, it was a strong week 1 turnout with three pairs tackling Montebello's slopes. And it was the Hills taking the W over the Van Kaenels and the Skidmore pair of Ammon and Adela. Special mention to Ammon who had to run to the finish line pulling his bike and Adela's trailer after he flatted a substantial distance from the line.

Among hybrid-electrics, it was a battle royale as Bill Bushnell finished first in 18:04, his second-best Low-Key time, finishing with his custom-build recumbent ahead of Rich Karlgaard on his Specialized hybrid.

In the all-important team competition, it's obviously very early days, but Team Brown Zone moved out to take the lead over the Pen Velo and San Jose Bike Club, but it's very, very close.
