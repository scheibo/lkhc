Report

And another year of Low-Key Hillclimbs comes to an end!

As usual, the podium competitions this year came down to the final climb, Mount Hamilton. In the men's division, Bill Laddish was able to mark Stefano Profumo to lock down his overall victory. David Collet wasn't able to make Mount Hamilton and ended up third. Chris Evans, despite his super-strong Hamilton result, wasn't able to fully close the gap. Zach Ulissi, hidden in the standings, fell one climb short of contending for the overall win with his 4 super-solid weekly scores.

In the women's competition, Janet Gardner's late-season injury prevented her from contesting the last two climbs. However, she had a strong enough buffer to hold on to the overall #1 position ahead of a very strong Shaena Berlin and Low-Key rookie Sindy Cho.

In the tandem division, it was the Dixons, with their very strong ride in the cold of Hamilton, just holding off Paul McKenzie whose ride with Sarah Schroer almost gave him enough to take the top spot. Stuart Taylor was next.

On the hybrid electric overall, Bill Bushnell won for the tenth year in a row, despite a potentially strong battle from Carol Ordemann, Carol impeded by various mechanical issues and a wrist injury.

In the runner's overall, Low-Key newbie and experienced ultra-runner John Burton. Bill Bushnell finished second with his fast hike of Purisima. Gary "the" Gellin, another super-fast ultra-runner, was third with his run of OLH.

The top female runner was once again Lisa Penzel, ahead of Angela Wilson.

In the team competition, it was Sisters and Misters ahead of Low-Key and a strength-by-numbers LGBRC. LGBRC dominated the megateam competition.

LGBRC also did very well in the all-important most-improved rider competition, with Bonnie Denoyer and Jeremy Scott showing strong point gains in what was a very hard year to improve (given the strong turnout of fast riders making for a "faster" average). Stephen Fong was a well-deserved third place.

Shahram Moatazedi, in addition to being very strong this year, was also most consistent.

Our top weekly score this year was Amy Cameron, with junior Illi Gardner second and Katy Giles result at Hamilton giving her the third best score for this year. So it was a women's 1-2-3 in this competition, with Bill Laddish the top men's score.

Kevin Metcalfe had the best age-adjusted score for the year. That's hardly a surprise.

Chris Evans was the best in the "mass-adjusted climbing" scores. This score is an estimate of pure climbing power. Carl Nielson and David Collet were 2nd and 3rd.

And finally, it was an elite 5 in our 100% club. Well done guys in supporting Low-Key Hillclimbs 2015 week after week!

Thanks to all of our riders and especially our coordinators and volunteers for making this year not only possible but an actual super-success!

Check back here for a future announcement of Low-Key Hillclimbs 2016, and also for information on on the MegaMonster Enduro on 6 Feb.

And please don't forget our long-time Low-Key partner Pen Velo and their classic New Year's Day San Bruno Hillclimb on 1 Jan, 2016!
