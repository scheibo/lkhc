Report

Road work from the week before proved to be a non-issue as Highway 9 was in excellent condition for Low-Key 2016 week 2. Add in the perfect weather, the spectacular organization of Lynn and Joe, and it was another perfect week of Low-Key goodness.

Low-Key was honored today with Ned Overend, clearly one of the most accomplished bike racers in US history, riding his Specialized Venge. And he proved that Specialized "aero is everything" machine isn't such a dog on the climbs either with his strong second place, a result which produced the best male age-adjusted score in Low-Key history. Todd Markhelz, who launched the group 1-splitting attack only a mile into the race, won, with Nick Wallace third and Hanns Detlefsen, rumoured to have been sequestered in monastary, emerged to score a super-strong fourth. It was really a packed field in group 1 this week. Todd's time blew away the existing Hwy 9 Low-Key record: it was smokin' fast out there this morning.

Among the women it was another super-strong turnout; 2016 is a strong women's year for Low-Key. The top woman today was Jenny Phillips, followed by Marty Scott and Katie Quinn.

In the runner's division we had a big turnout of two as Dan Connelly, and Adam Tow started the climb together, leaving soon after group 1. Dan set out at a solid but clearly unsustainable pace, but after the first 3, flatter kilometers, progressively faded until crossing the finish line in a truly sorry state. By that point, Adam had overtaken him, running a smartly paced effort for the win, Adam's first in a long Low-Key history. Dan was overheard muttering semi-coherently about "his cold" and "not having been able to eat dinner last night", but at Low-Key excuses are neither given nor accepted. It was Adam carrying the day and Adam now leads the overall rankings in the running division, with Dan in last place.

In hybrid electric it was Bill Bushnell once again taking the victory with an excellent showing, proving his installation of a direct drive motor this year hasn't hurt his hill-climbing prowess at all.

Easy Riders continued their domination of the team rankings, with Sisters and Misters second and LGBRC third.
