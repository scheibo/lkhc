Report

Wow! What an amazing finish to the 2011 Low-Key Hillclimbs, with all three major rankings changing on the final climb: congratulations to Brian Lucido, Lisa Penzel, and Sisters and Misters of No Mercy for taking the top spots! Free entries to the San Bruno Hillclimb on 1 Jan go to the top 3 men and top 3 women in the overall: congratulations to Brian, Tracy, Carl, Lisa, Helen, and McLovely! And a spot also goes to the top junior, Riley Auten: congratulations, Riley! Winning the Low-Key junior competition has led to lead to pro contracts... not to put any pressure on Riley.

In the hybrid-electric division, Bill Bushnell continued his perfect record with his sixth consecutive win here. Bill was also a huge contributor to the series, helping even on days he rode.

In the Endurance Award, Luis Valenta stuck to it all year, winning this award by a solid 38+ minutes over second place Jan Berka. Congrats to Luis: he'll get one of our free entries to the San Bruno Hillclimb on 1 Jan.

McLovely the very deserving winner of the most-improved ranking during the series. She volunteered for the early climbs until she was able to ride again, then from there improved every time she came out. This ranking tends to produce some unusual results and will probably be retired next year, but this year it certainly worked nicely. Ironically, she also wins the "most consistent rider" competition.

Most improved since last year was Bill Bushnell.... hmmm. But top spot among human powered riders was Skyler Colwell. Once again, the numbers give results we could have predicted. Skyler improved dramatically since last year, gaining confidence as well as strength, endurance, and speed. Very nice riding, Mr. Colwell!

Top score for the series was Jesse Miller-Smith (Sierra), but the top rated rider (after adjusting for who showed up each week, a preview of the 2012 scoring system) was Melanie Spath, thanks to her super-impressive climbing on Hamilton. Melanie had the top overall score in 2010.

New this year was the mass-adjusted rider score, multiplying best-for-the-year rate of climbing by self-reported rider mass. Ryan Sherlock wins this one ahead of Brian Lucido and Dave Russow.

Thanks to all the riders who came out this year, an amazingly successful Low-Key Hillclimb series! We'll have some changes next year, so stay tuned for those!
